Most of my projects start the same way. I read something interesting, get
excited about it, and then forget most of the details within a week — only to
rediscover the same idea months later as if it were new. I wanted a better way
to hold on to what I learn, so I started building **memex**.

## The idea behind the name

The name is a reference to Vannevar Bush. In his 1945 essay *As We May Think*,
Bush described the **memex**: a desk-sized machine where a person could store
their books, notes, and records, and link them together by association — closer
to how the mind actually moves from one idea to the next. Instead of folders and
filenames, he imagined **trails** of connected thought that you could follow
again later.

It was a remarkable idea for its time, and in some ways our tools still haven't
caught up. We have nearly unlimited storage, but recall is still hard.

## What I'm building

memex is my attempt at a small, personal version of that idea. The goals are
simple to state, even if they're not simple to build:

- **Capture quickly** — notes, links, and snippets, without friction
- **Connect by association** — relationships between ideas, not rigid hierarchy
- **Resurface at the right time** — bring things back when they're useful again

I'm not trying to build another note-taking app. I want a tool that helps me
think and remember, rather than just storing things I'll never look at again.

## Where the project stands

This is an early progress update, not a finished product:

- The core concept and overall shape of the project are settled.
- The capture and storage layer is in progress.
- The model for linking ideas together is the next major piece of work.
- Resurfacing and the interface are still in the design stage.

I'll keep writing about the progress here — including the dead ends and the
things that don't work — as the project develops. More soon.
