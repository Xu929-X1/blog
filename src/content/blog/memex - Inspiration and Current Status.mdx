## ► START

Every project I build seems to start the same way: I read something, get excited,
forget 90% of it within a week, then rediscover the same idea months later like
it's brand new. I got tired of being the bottleneck in my own memory.

So I started building **memex**.

## The 1945 idea

The name is a nod to Vannevar Bush. In his 1945 essay *As We May Think*, he
imagined the **memex** — a desk-sized machine where a person could store all
their books, notes, and records, then link them together by association, the way
the human mind actually jumps from idea to idea. Not folders. Not a search box.
**Trails** of connected thought you could follow later.

It was decades ahead of its time, and honestly, most of our tools today still
don't deliver on it. We have infinite storage and terrible recall.

## What I'm actually building

memex is my attempt at a small, personal version of that idea:

- Capture things fast — notes, links, snippets — without ceremony
- Connect them by association, not rigid hierarchy
- Resurface the right thing at the right moment, instead of letting it rot

The goal isn't another note app. It's a tool that makes *future-me* smarter by
remembering on my behalf.

## Current status

This is an early checkpoint, not a victory screen:

- ✅ Core idea and shape of the project locked in
- 🔨 Capture + storage layer in progress
- 🕹️ Association / linking model — next boss fight
- 🔒 Resurfacing + UI — still in the level select screen

I'll be posting progress, dead ends, and the occasional small win here as I go.

> If a machine can help us think, the least we can do is build it carefully.
